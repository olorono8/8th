‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is adolescence?

Adolescence is the period of life between childhood and adulthood, usually from 11 to 18 years of age.

2. What is puberty?

Puberty is the stage when the body undergoes physical and hormonal changes leading to sexual maturity.

3. What are secondary sexual characters?

Features that appear during puberty such as facial hair in boys and breast development in girls.

4. What are hormones?

Hormones are chemical substances secreted by endocrine glands that control various body functions.

5. Which hormone is responsible for changes in males during puberty?

Testosterone is responsible for male puberty changes.

6. Which hormone is responsible for changes in females during puberty?

Estrogen is responsible for female puberty changes.

7. What is the function of the pituitary gland?

The pituitary gland is the master gland that controls the secretion of hormones from other glands.

8. Why is adolescence considered a crucial stage?

It is the stage where physical, emotional, and mental changes occur, preparing a person for adulthood.

9. What is the role of nutrients during adolescence?

Proper nutrition is needed for growth, development, and maintaining energy during adolescence.

10. What is the menstrual cycle?

The menstrual cycle is the monthly cycle in females involving the shedding of the uterine lining, controlled by hormones.
