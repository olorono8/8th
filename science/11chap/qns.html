‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is force?

A force is a push or a pull on an object that can change its state of motion or shape.

2. Name the types of forces.

Contact forces (e.g. muscular, friction) and non-contact forces (e.g. magnetic, electrostatic, gravitational).

3. What is a contact force?

A contact force is a force that requires physical contact to act on an object. Example: Muscular force.

4. What is a non-contact force?

A non-contact force is a force that can act on an object without touching it. Example: Magnetic force.

5. What are the effects of force?

Force can move a body, stop a moving body, change speed or direction, and change the shape of an object.

6. What is pressure?

Pressure is the force applied per unit area. It is measured in Pascals (Pa).

7. How is pressure calculated?

Pressure = Force √∑ Area.

8. Why do sharp objects cut better?

Sharp objects have a small surface area, so pressure is higher even with the same force.

9. What is atmospheric pressure?

The pressure exerted by the weight of the air in the atmosphere on all objects is called atmospheric pressure.

10. Name some applications of pressure in daily life.

Hydraulic lift, drinking through a straw, sucking with a syringe, cutting with a knife.

üìù Extra Notes / Definitions

Force: A push or pull that can change motion or shape.
Contact Force: Acts through physical contact (e.g., muscular force).
Non-contact Force: Acts without contact (e.g., magnetic, gravitational).
Muscular Force: Force applied using body muscles.
Friction: Force that opposes motion between surfaces.
Magnetic Force: Attraction or repulsion by magnets.
Gravitational Force: Attraction between objects due to mass.
Pressure: Force per unit area (P = F √∑ A).
Atmospheric Pressure: Pressure exerted by air around us.
Hydraulic System: System using liquid pressure to lift or move objects.
