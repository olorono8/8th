‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is friction?

Friction is the force that opposes the motion of one surface over another.

2. What causes friction?

Friction is caused by the irregularities on the surfaces in contact.

3. List types of friction.

Static friction, sliding friction, rolling friction, and fluid friction.

4. Which is greater: static or sliding friction?

Static friction is greater than sliding friction.

5. Why is it easier to roll an object than to slide it?

Because rolling friction is less than sliding friction.

6. What is fluid friction?

Friction experienced by objects moving through a fluid (liquid or gas) is called fluid friction or drag.

7. How can friction be reduced?

By using lubricants, ball bearings, smooth surfaces, or converting sliding friction to rolling friction.

8. Is friction always harmful?

No, friction is both helpful (e.g., walking, writing) and harmful (e.g., wear and tear).

9. What are lubricants?

Lubricants are substances like oil or grease that reduce friction between surfaces.

10. What is streamlined shape?

A streamlined shape is a smooth, narrow shape designed to reduce fluid friction, used in cars, planes, and boats.

üìù Extra Notes / Definitions

Friction: Force that opposes motion between two surfaces.
Static Friction: Friction when an object is at rest.
Sliding Friction: Friction when an object is sliding over another.
Rolling Friction: Friction when an object rolls over a surface.
Fluid Friction (Drag): Friction when an object moves through a fluid.
Lubricant: A substance used to reduce friction.
Streamlined Shape: Shape that reduces drag in fluids.
Advantages of Friction: Helps in walking, holding objects, braking.
Disadvantages of Friction: Causes wear, produces unwanted heat.
