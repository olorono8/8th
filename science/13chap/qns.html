‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is sound?

Sound is a form of energy produced by vibrating objects and travels as waves through a medium.

2. How does sound travel?

Sound travels as longitudinal waves through solids, liquids, and gases by vibrating particles.

3. What is pitch?

Pitch is how high or low a sound is, determined by the frequency of the sound waves.

4. What is loudness?

Loudness is the intensity of sound, related to the amplitude of the sound waves.

5. What is the speed of sound in air?

The speed of sound in air at room temperature is approximately 343 meters per second.

6. What is echo?

Echo is the reflected sound that is heard after the original sound has stopped.

7. What is reverberation?

Reverberation is the persistence of sound due to multiple reflections in a closed space.

8. What is sonar?

Sonar is a technique that uses sound waves to detect underwater objects and measure water depth.

9. How do humans hear sound?

Sound waves enter the ear, cause the eardrum to vibrate, and these vibrations are converted into nerve signals sent to the brain.

10. Can sound travel in vacuum?

No, sound cannot travel in a vacuum because it needs a medium to propagate.

üìù Extra Notes / Definitions

Sound Wave: Longitudinal wave produced by vibrating objects.
Frequency: Number of vibrations per second, measured in Hertz (Hz).
Amplitude: Height of the wave, related to loudness.
Echo: Reflected sound heard after the original.
Reverberation: Persistence of sound due to repeated reflections.
Sonar: Sound navigation and ranging technology.
Pitch: Highness or lowness of sound determined by frequency.
Loudness: Perceived intensity of sound.
