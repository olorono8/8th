üîé Introduction

Electric current passing through certain liquids causes chemical reactions. This is called the chemical effect of electric current.

Chemical Effect of Electric Current: The effect in which electric current causes chemical changes in a substance.

‚ö°Ô∏è Electrolysis

When electric current passes through an electrolyte (a liquid that conducts electricity), it causes the electrolyte to decompose into its components.

This process is called electrolysis.

Example: Passing current through water mixed with acid or salt breaks it into hydrogen and oxygen gases.

‚öôÔ∏è Components of an Electrolytic Cell

  • Electrolyte: The conducting liquid or solution.
  • Electrodes: Two metal rods placed in the electrolyte connected to the battery; one is positive (anode) and the other negative (cathode).
  • Battery: Provides the electric current.

‚ûï Anode and Cathode

  • Anode: The positive electrode where oxidation occurs (loss of electrons).
  • Cathode: The negative electrode where reduction occurs (gain of electrons).

üß™ Examples of Electrolysis

  • Electrolysis of water: Produces hydrogen gas at cathode and oxygen gas at anode.
  • Electroplating: Depositing a layer of metal on an object to prevent corrosion or for decoration (e.g., silver plating on jewelry).
  • Extraction of metals: Metals like aluminum are extracted from their ores by electrolysis.

üß¨ Uses of Chemical Effects of Electric Current

  • Purification of metals like copper.
  • Electroplating to coat metals for protection and decoration.
  • Production of chemicals like chlorine, hydrogen, and sodium hydroxide.

‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Precautions

  • Handle electric currents carefully to avoid shocks.
  • Use proper insulation and equipment while performing electrolysis.
  • Electrolytic experiments should be done under adult supervision.

üåü Key Points to Remember

  • Electric current can cause chemical changes in solutions, called chemical effects.
  • Electrolysis decomposes electrolytes into simpler substances.
  • Anode is positive electrode; cathode is negative electrode.
  • Electroplating uses chemical effects of electric current for metal coating.
  • Electrolysis is used in metal extraction, purification, and chemical production.
