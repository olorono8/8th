‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is electric current?

Electric current is the flow of electric charge through a conductor.

2. What is electrolysis?

Electrolysis is the chemical decomposition caused by passing an electric current through a liquid or solution containing ions.

3. What are electrodes?

Electrodes are conductors through which electric current enters or leaves the electrolyte during electrolysis.

4. What is an electrolyte?

An electrolyte is a substance that conducts electricity when dissolved in water, allowing electrolysis to occur.

5. What happens at the cathode during electrolysis?

At the cathode, positively charged ions gain electrons and are reduced to form neutral atoms or molecules.

6. What happens at the anode during electrolysis?

At the anode, negatively charged ions lose electrons and are oxidized.

7. What is electroplating?

Electroplating is the process of depositing a layer of metal on an object by electrolysis.

8. Give one example of electroplating.

Silver plating of spoons is a common example of electroplating.

9. How can electric current cause chemical changes?

Electric current causes ions in the electrolyte to move and react at the electrodes, producing chemical changes.

10. Why is electrolysis important?

Electrolysis is important for extracting metals, purifying metals, and electroplating.
