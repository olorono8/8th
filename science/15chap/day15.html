üå©Ô∏è Introduction

Natural phenomena such as lightning, thunder, earthquakes, and magnetic effects occur due to natural forces and processes on Earth.

‚ö° Lightning and Thunder

Lightning is a sudden discharge of electricity in the atmosphere during a thunderstorm.

  • It occurs due to the buildup of electrical charges in clouds.
  • When the charges become too large, electricity flows between clouds or from clouds to the ground as lightning.
  • Thunder is the sound produced by the rapid expansion of air caused by lightning.

Safety tips: During thunderstorms, avoid open areas and tall trees. Stay indoors if possible.

üå™Ô∏è Cyclones

Cyclones are violent storms characterized by strong winds and heavy rains.

  • They form over warm ocean waters.
  • Cyclones rotate around a low-pressure center.
  • They can cause widespread damage due to winds, storm surges, and flooding.

People in cyclone-prone areas are advised to follow weather warnings and evacuate if needed.

üåç Earthquakes

An earthquake is the shaking of the Earth's surface caused by the sudden release of energy from the Earth's crust.

  • It is caused by the movement of tectonic plates.
  • The point inside Earth where an earthquake starts is called the focus.
  • The point on the surface directly above the focus is called the epicenter.
  • Earthquakes can cause damage to buildings, roads, and can trigger tsunamis.

Safety tips: During an earthquake, drop to the ground, take cover under a sturdy table, and hold on until shaking stops.

üß≠ Magnetic Effects of Electric Current

Electric currents produce magnetic fields around them, which can attract or repel magnets.

  • A current-carrying wire behaves like a magnet.
  • This principle is used in devices like electromagnets, electric bells, and motors.

Electromagnets can be switched on and off, making them very useful in many electrical devices.

üîë Key Points to Remember

  • Lightning is caused by the discharge of electric charges during thunderstorms.
  • Thunder is the sound caused by lightning heating the air.
  • Cyclones are strong storms formed over oceans with rotating winds.
  • Earthquakes result from movements of tectonic plates.
  • Electric current produces magnetic fields used in electromagnets.
  • Safety precautions during natural disasters help save lives.
