‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What causes lightning?

Lightning is caused by the discharge of static electricity during a thunderstorm.

2. What is thunder?

Thunder is the sound produced by the rapid expansion of air due to the heat of lightning.

3. How can we protect ourselves during a thunderstorm?

By staying indoors, avoiding tall trees and metal objects, and not using electrical appliances.

4. What is the role of a lightning conductor?

A lightning conductor safely directs lightning current to the ground, protecting buildings from damage.

5. What is an earthquake?

An earthquake is the shaking of the Earth's surface caused by sudden movements in the Earth's crust.

6. What causes earthquakes?

Earthquakes are caused by tectonic plate movements, volcanic activity, or human activities like mining.

7. How do we measure earthquakes?

Earthquakes are measured using a seismograph, and their intensity is expressed on the Richter scale.

8. What safety measures should be taken during an earthquake?

Stay under sturdy furniture, away from glass and heavy objects, and move to open areas if outdoors.

9. What is the difference between lightning and electricity?

Lightning is a natural electrical discharge in the atmosphere, while electricity is the flow of electric charge used in devices.

10. Why do electric sparks occur?

Electric sparks occur when electrons jump across a gap between charged objects, ionizing the air and creating light.
