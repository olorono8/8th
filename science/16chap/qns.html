‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is light?

Light is a form of energy that enables us to see objects by reflecting off them and entering our eyes.

2. What are the sources of light?

Sources of light include the Sun, electric bulbs, candles, and fireflies.

3. What is reflection of light?

Reflection of light is the bouncing back of light rays when they strike a smooth surface.

4. What is the difference between regular and diffused reflection?

Regular reflection occurs on smooth surfaces and produces clear images; diffused reflection occurs on rough surfaces and does not produce clear images.

5. What is the law of reflection?

The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.

6. What is a plane mirror?

A plane mirror is a flat mirror that reflects light to form an image.

7. What are the characteristics of images formed by a plane mirror?

The image is virtual, erect, laterally inverted, and of the same size as the object.

8. What is refraction of light?

Refraction is the bending of light when it passes from one medium to another.

9. What causes a straw in a glass of water to appear bent?

The bending of light (refraction) causes the straw to appear bent at the water surface.

10. What is total internal reflection?

Total internal reflection occurs when light hits the boundary of a denser medium to a rarer medium at an angle greater than the critical angle and is completely reflected inside.

üìù Extra Notes / Definitions

Light: A form of energy visible to the human eye.
Reflection: Bouncing back of light from a surface.
Refraction: Bending of light as it passes through different media.
Plane Mirror: A flat mirror producing virtual images.
Virtual Image: An image formed when rays appear to come from a point behind the mirror.
Total Internal Reflection: Complete reflection inside a denser medium.
Critical Angle: Minimum angle of incidence for total internal reflection.
