‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is a star?

A star is a huge ball of hot, glowing gases that produces its own light and heat through nuclear reactions.

2. What is the solar system?

The solar system consists of the Sun and all the objects that orbit it, including planets, moons, asteroids, and comets.

3. Name the planets of the solar system in order from the Sun.

Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

4. What is a galaxy?

A galaxy is a huge collection of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter bound together by gravity. The Milky Way is our galaxy.

5. What causes day and night?

Day and night are caused by the Earth‚Äôs rotation on its axis, exposing different parts to sunlight.

6. What is a comet?

A comet is a small icy body that orbits the Sun, often developing a glowing tail when close to the Sun.

7. What is the difference between a planet and a moon?

A planet orbits the Sun directly, while a moon orbits a planet.

8. What is an asteroid?

Asteroids are small rocky bodies that orbit the Sun, mainly found between Mars and Jupiter in the asteroid belt.

9. How does the Sun produce energy?

The Sun produces energy through nuclear fusion, where hydrogen atoms fuse to form helium, releasing huge amounts of energy.

10. What is gravity‚Äôs role in the solar system?

Gravity keeps the planets, moons, and other objects in orbit around the Sun.
