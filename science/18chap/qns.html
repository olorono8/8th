‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is air pollution?

Air pollution is the presence of harmful substances in the air that can cause health problems and damage the environment.

2. Name some common air pollutants.

Smoke, dust, carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and harmful gases from vehicles and industries.

3. What are the effects of air pollution on health?

It can cause respiratory problems, asthma, bronchitis, and other lung diseases.

4. What causes water pollution?

Water pollution is caused by discharge of untreated sewage, industrial waste, chemical fertilizers, and pesticides into water bodies.

5. How does water pollution affect aquatic life?

It kills fish and other aquatic organisms by reducing oxygen levels and introducing toxic substances.

6. What is acid rain?

Acid rain is rain containing harmful acids formed when sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides mix with water vapor in the atmosphere.

7. How can air pollution be controlled?

By reducing the use of fossil fuels, using cleaner fuels, planting trees, and controlling industrial emissions.

8. What steps can be taken to reduce water pollution?

Treating industrial and sewage waste before releasing it, avoiding use of harmful chemicals near water bodies, and practicing water conservation.

9. What is smog?

Smog is a type of air pollution caused by the reaction of sunlight with pollutants like smoke and fog.

10. Why is clean water important?

Clean water is essential for drinking, hygiene, agriculture, and maintaining healthy ecosystems.

Air Pollution: Contamination of air by harmful substances.
Water Pollution: Contamination of water bodies with pollutants.
Acid Rain: Rainfall made acidic by atmospheric pollutants.
Smog: Fog mixed with smoke and pollutants.
Sewage: Wastewater from homes and industries.
Fossil Fuels: Non-renewable energy sources like coal and petrol.
Respiratory Diseases: Illnesses affecting lungs and breathing.
