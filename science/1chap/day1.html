üå± Introduction

In India, farming is a major activity. Plants grown on a large scale for food and raw materials are called crops.

Crop: Plants of the same kind grown in a field on a large scale.

Examples of crops:

  • Kharif Crops (Rainy Season): Rice, Maize, Cotton
  • Rabi Crops (Winter Season): Wheat, Mustard, Gram

üîÑ Basic Practices of Crop Production

Farming is done in steps. Let‚Äôs understand each one:

Preparation of Soil ‚Üí Sowing ‚Üí Adding Manure/Fertilizer ‚Üí Irrigation ‚Üí Weeding ‚Üí Harvesting ‚Üí Storage

1. Preparation of Soil

The soil is loosened using a plough so that roots can breathe, and nutrients mix well.

2. Sowing

Healthy seeds are sown at the right depth and distance. Modern tools like the seed drill are used.

3. Adding Manure and Fertilizers

Plants need nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus. We use:

  • Manure: Natural, organic; improves soil texture.
  • Fertilizer: Chemical-based; faster nutrient supply.

4. Irrigation

Supplying water at right time is crucial. Methods:

  • Traditional: Moat, Dhekli, Rahat
  • Modern: Sprinkler and Drip system (water-saving)

5. Weeding

Unwanted plants called weeds compete for nutrients and must be removed using weedicides or manually.

6. Harvesting

When crops mature, they are cut and collected using sickles or harvesters.

7. Storage

Grains are dried and stored in jute bags, bins, or silos to protect from pests and moisture.

  • Plough: For loosening soil.

ü¶† Crop Protection

Crops are often attacked by pests, insects, and fungi. Farmers use:

  • Pesticides to kill insects.
  • Fungicides for fungal infections.
  • Weedicides for weed control.

üß† Key Points to Remember

  • Crops are classified as Kharif and Rabi based on season.
  • 7 major steps in farming: Soil prep ‚Üí Sowing ‚Üí Manuring ‚Üí Irrigation ‚Üí Weeding ‚Üí Harvesting ‚Üí Storage
  • Manure is natural; fertilizer is chemical.
  • Weeds harm crops and must be removed regularly.
  • Proper storage prevents loss due to pests and moisture.
