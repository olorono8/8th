1. What is agriculture?

Agriculture is the practice of growing crops and raising animals for food and other products.

2. Define crops. Give two examples.

Crops are plants of the same kind grown and cultivated at one place on a large scale. Example: Wheat, Rice.

3. What are Kharif and Rabi crops?

Kharif crops are sown in the rainy season (June‚ÄìSeptember), e.g., rice, maize.
Rabi crops are sown in the winter season (October‚ÄìMarch), e.g., wheat, mustard.

4. List the basic steps of crop production.

Preparation of soil ‚Üí Sowing ‚Üí Adding manure/fertilizers ‚Üí Irrigation ‚Üí Weeding ‚Üí Harvesting ‚Üí Storage

5. What is the importance of ploughing?

Ploughing loosens the soil, improves air circulation, and helps roots grow better.

6. What is irrigation? Name two modern methods.

Irrigation is the artificial supply of water to crops. Two modern methods are: Sprinkler system and Drip system.

7. What is weeding? Name one weedicides.

Weeding is the removal of unwanted plants (weeds) from crop fields. Example of weedicide: 2,4-D.

8. Why is storage important?

Storage prevents damage by moisture, pests, and microorganisms. Grains are dried and stored in silos or granaries.

9. What is the use of manure and fertilizers?

They add nutrients to the soil and increase crop yield. Manure is organic; fertilizers are chemical-based.

üìù Extra Notes / Definitions

Plough: A tool used for tilling soil before sowing.
Seed Drill: A device used to sow seeds at proper depth and spacing.
Fertilizers: Chemicals that supply nutrients to the soil quickly.
Manure: Natural fertilizer made from animal or plant waste.
Harvesting: Cutting and gathering of mature crops from the field.
Storage: Keeping harvested grains safe from damage in dry and pest-free conditions.
Rabi Crops: Grown in winter, harvested in spring. E.g., wheat, peas.
Kharif Crops: Grown in rainy season. E.g., rice, maize.
