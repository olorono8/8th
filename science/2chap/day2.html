üî¨ What are Microorganisms?

Microorganisms are tiny living organisms that cannot be seen with the naked eye. They are found everywhere: in air, water, soil‚Äîeven inside our bodies!

Microorganisms: Microscopic organisms such as bacteria, fungi, protozoa, algae, and viruses.

Major Groups:

  • Bacteria
  • Fungi
  • Protozoa
  • Algae
  • Viruses (need a host to reproduce)

üòä Useful Microorganisms

  • Lactobacillus: Converts milk to curd.
  • Yeast: Used in baking and alcohol production (fermentation).
  • Bacteria: Fix nitrogen in soil (Rhizobium), produce vitamins.
  • Microbes: Used in making antibiotics like penicillin.
  • Vaccines: Prepared using weak microbes to prevent diseases.
üß™ Example: Yeast + Sugar = Alcohol + CO‚ÇÇ (Fermentation)

‚ò£Ô∏è Harmful Microorganisms

Some microbes cause diseases in humans, animals, and plants. They also spoil food.

  • Diseases in Humans: Cholera, Tuberculosis, Measles, COVID-19 (virus)
  • Diseases in Animals: Foot and mouth disease in cattle
  • Diseases in Plants: Citrus canker, Rust of wheat
  • Food Spoilage: Caused by bacteria and fungi
Pathogens: Disease-causing microorganisms.

üíâ Food Preservation Techniques

To prevent spoilage by microorganisms, we use various methods:

  • Refrigeration: Slows down growth
  • Salting and Sugar: Removes moisture (used in pickles, jams)
  • Boiling: Kills microbes
  • Chemicals: Sodium benzoate, preservatives
  • Canning: Airtight packaging

üß¨ Nitrogen Cycle

Microorganisms help in maintaining the balance of nitrogen in nature.

  • Fixation: Bacteria like Rhizobium fix nitrogen from air into soil.
  • Decomposition: Converts dead organisms into nitrates.
  • Plants absorb nitrates and animals eat plants.
  • Denitrification: Some bacteria return nitrogen to the atmosphere.
üå± Without microbes, plants cannot get nitrogen!

üß† Key Points to Remember

  • Microorganisms are everywhere‚Äîsome are helpful, others are harmful.
  • They are used in curd, bread, alcohol, medicine (antibiotics), and vaccines.
  • They cause diseases in plants, animals, and humans.
  • Preservation methods prevent food spoilage.
  • They play a major role in the nitrogen cycle.
