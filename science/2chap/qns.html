1. What are microorganisms?

Microorganisms are tiny living organisms that cannot be seen with the naked eye. They include bacteria, viruses, fungi, protozoa, and algae.

2. Name five groups of microorganisms.

Bacteria, Viruses, Fungi, Protozoa, and Algae.

3. How are microorganisms useful?

They help in making curd, bread, alcohol, antibiotics, vaccines, and nitrogen fixation in soil.

4. Name some diseases caused by microorganisms.

Cholera, Tuberculosis, Typhoid, Influenza, Polio, COVID-19.

5. What are pathogens?

Pathogens are microorganisms that cause diseases in humans, animals, or plants.

6. How can food be preserved from spoilage?

By boiling, refrigeration, adding preservatives, drying, salting, or storing in airtight containers (canning).

7. What is fermentation? Which microorganism is used?

Fermentation is the conversion of sugar into alcohol and CO‚ÇÇ by yeast. It is used in baking and brewing.

8. What is the nitrogen cycle?

It is the process in which nitrogen is converted into different forms and circulated between air, soil, plants, and animals with the help of microorganisms.

9. What is vaccination?

Vaccination is the process of giving a weakened microbe into the body to develop immunity against a disease.

Microorganism: A very small living thing that cannot be seen without a microscope.
Lactobacillus: A bacteria that turns milk into curd.
Fermentation: Breakdown of sugar by yeast to produce alcohol.
Preservation: Methods used to prevent food spoilage.
Pathogens: Microorganisms that cause disease.
Vaccine: A substance made of weakened microbes to build immunity.
Nitrogen Fixation: Process of converting atmospheric nitrogen into usable form by bacteria like Rhizobium.
Antibiotic: A medicine that kills or stops the growth of disease-causing bacteria.
