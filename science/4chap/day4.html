üß™ Introduction

Everything around us is made of matter, which can be broadly divided into metals and non-metals. Metals are generally hard, shiny, and good conductors of heat and electricity. Non-metals have contrasting properties.

Metal: Elements that are usually shiny, malleable, ductile, and good conductors of heat and electricity.
Non-Metal: Elements that are usually dull, brittle, and poor conductors of heat and electricity.

üîç Physical Properties of Metals

  • Shiny appearance: Metals have a lustrous surface when polished.
  • Good conductors: Metals conduct heat and electricity well.
  • Malleability: Metals can be hammered into thin sheets.
  • Ductility: Metals can be drawn into wires.
  • Sonorous: Metals produce a ringing sound when struck.
  • High melting and boiling points: Most metals melt and boil at high temperatures.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Physical Properties of Non-Metals

  • Dull appearance: Non-metals are generally not shiny.
  • Poor conductors: Non-metals do not conduct heat or electricity well.
  • Brittle: Non-metals break easily when hammered.
  • Non-ductile and non-malleable: Cannot be drawn into wires or hammered into sheets.
  • Low melting and boiling points: Most non-metals melt and boil at lower temperatures.

‚öóÔ∏è Chemical Properties

Reaction with Oxygen

Most metals react with oxygen to form metal oxides.

  • Example: 2Mg + O2 ‚Üí 2MgO (Magnesium oxide)

Non-metals also react with oxygen to form non-metal oxides.

  • Example: S + O2 ‚Üí SO2 (Sulfur dioxide)

Reaction with Water

Some metals react with water to produce metal hydroxides and hydrogen gas.

  • Example: 2Na + 2H2O ‚Üí 2NaOH + H2

Non-metals generally do not react with water.

Reaction with Acids

Metals react with acids to produce salts and hydrogen gas.

  • Example: Zn + 2HCl ‚Üí ZnCl2 + H2

Non-metals usually do not react with acids.

üõ† Uses of Metals

  • Iron: Used in construction and manufacturing machines.
  • Copper: Used in electrical wiring and plumbing.
  • Aluminium: Used in aircraft, utensils, and packaging.
  • Gold and Silver: Used in jewelry and electronics.

‚ö†Ô∏è Uses of Non-Metals

  • Sulfur: Used in making matches and fertilizers.
  • Phosphorus: Used in safety matches and detergents.
  • Carbon: Used in pencils (graphite), and fuels (coal).
  • Oxygen: Essential for respiration and combustion.

üß† Key Points to Remember

  • Metals are shiny, malleable, ductile, and good conductors.
  • Non-metals are dull, brittle, and poor conductors.
  • Metals react with oxygen, acids, and sometimes water; non-metals react mostly with oxygen.
  • Uses of metals and non-metals vary based on their properties.
