‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is heat?

Heat is a form of energy that causes a rise in temperature and can be transferred from one body to another.

2. What is temperature?

Temperature is a measure of how hot or cold a body is, usually measured using a thermometer.

3. Define conduction with example.

Conduction is the transfer of heat through a solid without the movement of the solid itself. Example: heating one end of a metal rod.

4. What is convection?

Convection is the transfer of heat through liquids or gases by the movement of the heated particles from one place to another.

5. What is radiation?

Radiation is the transfer of heat through empty space by electromagnetic waves, like the heat from the Sun.

6. What is the role of a thermometer?

A thermometer measures temperature using a calibrated scale.

7. What is expansion and contraction?

Expansion is the increase in size of substances on heating, while contraction is the decrease in size on cooling.

8. Why do bridges have expansion joints?

To allow the bridge materials to expand and contract with temperature changes without causing damage.

9. What is specific heat capacity?

It is the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of 1 kg of a substance by 1¬∞C.
