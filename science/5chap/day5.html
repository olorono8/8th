üõ¢Ô∏è Introduction

Coal and petroleum are important fossil fuels used as energy sources worldwide. They are formed from the remains of dead plants and animals over millions of years.

Fossil Fuels: Fuels formed from the dead remains of plants and animals over millions of years.

ü™® Formation of Coal

Coal is formed from the remains of plants that lived in swampy forests millions of years ago. The process involves:

  • Dead plants accumulate in marshy areas forming peat.
  • Peat gets buried under soil and subjected to heat and pressure.
  • Over millions of years, peat transforms into coal through carbonization.

Types of coal based on carbon content:

  • Lignite: Brown coal, lowest carbon content.
  • Bituminous: Soft coal, medium carbon content.
  • Anthracite: Hard coal, highest carbon content and energy.

üõ¢Ô∏è Formation of Petroleum

Petroleum (crude oil) is formed from the remains of tiny sea plants and animals (plankton) that died millions of years ago and settled on the sea floor.

  • The remains get buried under layers of sand and silt.
  • Heat and pressure convert these remains into petroleum over millions of years.
Diagram showing petroleum formation under the Earth's surface.

‚õΩ Uses of Coal and Petroleum

  • Coal: Used as fuel in thermal power plants, in industries for producing steel, cement, and in households for heating and cooking.
  • Petroleum: Refined to get petrol, diesel, kerosene, lubricants, and other petrochemicals.
  • Petroleum products are also raw materials for plastics, synthetic fibers, and medicines.

üõ¢Ô∏è Refining of Petroleum

Petroleum is separated into useful components through a process called refining. The main steps are:

  • Distillation: Crude oil is heated; components with different boiling points are separated.
  • Conversion: Heavy fractions are converted into lighter, useful fuels.

‚ö†Ô∏è Environmental Concerns

Burning coal and petroleum releases greenhouse gases causing air pollution and global warming. Oil spills cause severe damage to marine life.

Efforts are ongoing to use cleaner energy and reduce fossil fuel consumption.

üß† Key Points to Remember

  • Coal and petroleum are fossil fuels formed from dead plants and animals.
  • Coal is mainly formed from plant remains in swamps; petroleum from tiny marine organisms.
  • Types of coal include lignite, bituminous, and anthracite.
  • Petroleum is refined to get fuels like petrol, diesel, and kerosene.
  • Fossil fuels cause pollution and efforts are needed to find alternatives.
