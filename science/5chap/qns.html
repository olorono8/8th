‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What are fossil fuels?

Fossil fuels are energy resources formed from the remains of dead plants and animals millions of years ago, such as coal and petroleum.

2. How is coal formed?

Coal is formed from the remains of plants that lived in swamps millions of years ago and got buried under layers of soil and rock, undergoing heat and pressure.

3. What is petroleum?

Petroleum is a dark, thick liquid fossil fuel formed from the remains of tiny sea animals and plants that settled on the seabed and got buried under sediment.

4. Name some products obtained from petroleum.

Petrol, diesel, kerosene, lubricating oil, paraffin wax, and plastics.

5. What is refining?

Refining is the process of separating petroleum into useful products by heating and distillation.

6. What are the advantages of using petroleum?

It is a versatile fuel used for transport, heating, electricity, and making plastics.

7. Why should we save fossil fuels?

Because they are non-renewable and take millions of years to form, excessive use can lead to depletion and environmental pollution.

8. What is natural gas?

Natural gas is a fossil fuel found along with petroleum, mainly composed of methane, used for cooking and electricity generation.

9. What are the environmental effects of burning fossil fuels?

Air pollution, acid rain, global warming due to release of greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide and sulfur dioxide.

üìù Extra Notes / Definitions

Fossil Fuels: Fuels formed from dead plants and animals over millions of years.
Coal: A black combustible rock formed from plant remains.
Petroleum: Liquid fossil fuel used to produce fuel and chemicals.
Refining: Process of separating petroleum into components.
Natural Gas: Gas found with petroleum, mainly methane.
Non-renewable: Cannot be replenished in a short time.
Distillation: Process to separate mixtures based on boiling points.
