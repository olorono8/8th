üî• What is Combustion?

Combustion is a chemical reaction where a substance reacts with oxygen to produce heat and light. It is commonly known as burning.

Combustion: A rapid chemical reaction between a fuel and oxygen that produces heat and light.

Example: When wood or coal burns, it undergoes combustion.

üïØ Types of Combustion

  • Rapid Combustion: Produces flame and heat (e.g., burning of LPG in stove).
  • Spontaneous Combustion: Occurs without external flame or spark (e.g., haystack catching fire).
  • Slow Combustion: Produces heat but no flame (e.g., rusting of iron).

üßØ Fire Extinguishing Methods

Fire can be put out by removing any one of the three things that fire needs:

  • Fuel: The material that burns.
  • Oxygen: Needed for combustion.
  • Heat: The ignition temperature.

Removing any one of these stops the fire.

Examples:

  • Throwing sand or using a fire extinguisher removes oxygen.
  • Water cools the heat.
  • Cutting off fuel stops the fire.

üïØ Structure of a Flame

A flame has three distinct zones:

  • Inner Zone: Dark, cold, unburnt gas.
  • Middle Zone: Luminous, yellow, partly burnt gases.
  • Outer Zone: Non-luminous, blue, completely burnt gases.
üì∏ The outer zone is hottest and best for heating.

‚õΩ Fuel and Combustion

Fuels are substances that burn in the presence of oxygen to produce heat.

  • Types of fuels: Solid (coal, wood), Liquid (petrol, kerosene), Gas (LPG, natural gas).
  • Good fuels should burn with a good flame, produce a lot of heat, and not leave much residue.

‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Measures

  • Keep flammable materials away from fire sources.
  • Use fire extinguishers or sand to put out fires.
  • Do not play with matches or lighters.
  • Ensure good ventilation when using gas appliances.

üß† Key Points to Remember

  • Combustion requires fuel, oxygen, and heat.
  • Removing any one of the three stops the fire.
  • Flame has three zones with different temperatures.
  • Fuels can be solid, liquid, or gas.
  • Fire safety is essential to prevent accidents.
