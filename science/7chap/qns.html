‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is deforestation?

Deforestation is the clearing or cutting down of forests by humans for agriculture, urbanization, or timber.

2. What are the consequences of deforestation?

It leads to soil erosion, loss of biodiversity, global warming, disturbance in the water cycle, and desertification.

3. What is biodiversity?

Biodiversity refers to the variety of different types of life forms like plants, animals, and microorganisms in a region.

4. What is a biosphere reserve?

A biosphere reserve is a protected area that conserves biodiversity and helps maintain the balance between humans and nature.

5. What is wildlife sanctuary?

It is a protected area where animals are safe from hunting and habitat destruction.

6. What is a national park?

A national park is a large protected area for the conservation of wildlife and their natural habitats.

7. Define endangered species.

Endangered species are those animals or plants that are at risk of extinction in the near future.

8. What is migration?

Migration is the seasonal movement of animals from one region to another for food, breeding, or climate conditions.

9. What is reforestation?

Reforestation is the process of planting trees in an area where forests were cut down earlier.

10. What is the role of forests in maintaining the balance in nature?

Forests maintain the water cycle, prevent soil erosion, purify air, and provide habitat to animals.
