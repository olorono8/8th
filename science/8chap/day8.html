üî¨ Introduction

The cell is the basic structural and functional unit of all living organisms. Whether an organism is made of a single cell or many cells, all life functions are carried out by cells. The discovery of cells was made possible by the invention of the microscope.

üìú Cell Theory

  • All living organisms are made up of one or more cells.
  • The cell is the smallest unit of life.
  • All cells arise from pre-existing cells.

üß´ Types of Cells

  • Prokaryotic cells: Simple cells without a defined nucleus. Example: bacteria.
  • Eukaryotic cells: Complex cells with a nucleus and membrane-bound organelles. Examples: plant and animal cells.

üß© Structure of a Typical Cell

Cells come in different shapes and sizes but usually have these main parts:

  • Cell Membrane: A thin, flexible layer that surrounds the cell, controls what enters and leaves the cell, and protects it.
  • Cytoplasm: Jelly-like substance inside the cell where organelles are suspended and many cell activities happen.
  • Nucleus: The control center of the cell that contains DNA and manages cell activities.

‚öôÔ∏è Cell Organelles and Their Functions

  • Mitochondria: Produces energy (ATP) for the cell; called the powerhouse of the cell.
  • Chloroplast: Found only in plant cells; contains chlorophyll and performs photosynthesis.
  • Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER): Transports materials inside the cell.
  • Ribosomes: Synthesizes proteins.
  • Golgi Apparatus: Packages and distributes proteins and lipids.
  • Lysosomes: Contains enzymes to digest waste and old cell parts.
  • Vacuoles: Stores water, nutrients, and waste; large in plant cells, small or absent in animal cells.
  • Cell Wall: Rigid outer layer in plant cells that provides structure and protection.

üîé Importance of Cells

  • Cells perform all life processes: nutrition, respiration, growth, reproduction, and excretion.
  • Studying cells helps us understand how organisms live and function.
  • Many diseases are caused by abnormalities in cells.

üî≠ Microscopes and Observation

The light microscope magnifies cells so we can see their structure. Stains and dyes help make parts of the cell visible.

üîÑ Cell Division

Cells divide to form new cells for growth, repair, and reproduction. There are two main types:

  • Mitosis: Produces identical cells for growth and repair.
  • Meiosis: Produces reproductive cells (gametes) with half the number of chromosomes.

üß† Key Points to Remember

  • The cell is the smallest unit of life.
  • Cells contain organelles that perform specific functions.
  • Plant and animal cells differ in certain features like cell wall and chloroplast.
  • Cells divide to form new cells, which is essential for life.
