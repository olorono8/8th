‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is a cell?

A cell is the basic structural and functional unit of all living organisms.

2. Who discovered the cell?

Robert Hooke discovered the cell in 1665.

3. What are unicellular organisms?

Organisms made up of a single cell, e.g., Amoeba and Paramecium.

4. Name the parts of a cell visible under a microscope.

Cell membrane, cytoplasm, and nucleus.

5. What is the function of the nucleus?

The nucleus controls all the activities of the cell and contains genetic material (DNA).

6. What is cytoplasm?

Cytoplasm is the jelly-like fluid between the nucleus and cell membrane where various cell organelles are found.

7. How do plant and animal cells differ?

Plant cells have a cell wall, large vacuole, and chloroplasts. Animal cells do not.

8. What is a cell membrane?

A thin, flexible outer layer of the cell that controls what enters and leaves the cell.

9. What is a vacuole?

A vacuole is a storage sac inside the cell that stores water, nutrients, and waste products.

10. What are cell organelles?

Cell organelles are small structures within a cell that perform specific functions like mitochondria, chloroplasts, etc.

üìù Extra Notes / Definitions

Cell: Basic unit of life.
Unicellular: Organisms made of one cell.
Multicellular: Organisms made of many cells.
Nucleus: Controls cell activities and contains DNA.
Cell Membrane: Outer covering that allows materials in and out.
Cytoplasm: Fluid that fills the cell and holds organelles.
Vacuole: Stores food, water, and waste.
Chloroplast: Organelle in plant cells where photosynthesis happens.
Cell Wall: Rigid outer layer in plant cells that provides shape and support.
