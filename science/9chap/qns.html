‚ùì Important Questions and Answers

1. What is reproduction?

Reproduction is the biological process by which living organisms produce new individuals of the same species.

2. What are the two main types of reproduction?

The two types are sexual reproduction and asexual reproduction.

3. What is sexual reproduction?

Sexual reproduction involves two parents and the fusion of male and female gametes (sperm and egg).

4. What is asexual reproduction?

Asexual reproduction involves only one parent and does not involve the fusion of gametes. Offspring are identical to the parent.

5. Name the male and female reproductive organs in humans.

Male: Testes, Penis; Female: Ovaries, Uterus, Vagina.

6. What are gametes?

Gametes are reproductive cells. Male gamete is sperm and female gamete is egg (ovum).

7. What is fertilization?

Fertilization is the fusion of male and female gametes to form a zygote.

8. What is the difference between internal and external fertilization?

Internal fertilization occurs inside the body (e.g., humans), while external fertilization occurs outside the body (e.g., frogs).

9. What is metamorphosis?

Metamorphosis is a biological process in which an animal undergoes a drastic change in body structure after birth or hatching. Example: Frog.

10. Name some animals that reproduce through budding and binary fission.

Budding: Hydra; Binary fission: Amoeba.

Reproduction: Process of producing offspring.
Sexual Reproduction: Involves two parents and gamete fusion.
Asexual Reproduction: Involves only one parent; no gametes.
Gametes: Reproductive cells ‚Äì sperm (male), egg (female).
Fertilization: Fusion of male and female gametes.
Zygote: Fertilized egg that develops into an embryo.
Internal Fertilization: Fertilization inside the female body.
External Fertilization: Fertilization outside the body, in water.
Metamorphosis: Major body changes during development.
Budding: Asexual reproduction where a new individual grows out of the parent (e.g., Hydra).
Binary Fission: Asexual reproduction where the cell splits into two (e.g., Amoeba).
